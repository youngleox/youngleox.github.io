---
Neural network performance predictors have great potential in reducing computation cost in neural architecture search (NAS) by reducing the need to fully train neural networks. We give the first large-scale study of performance predictors by analyzing 31 techniques ranging from learning curve extrapolation, to weight-sharing, to supervised learning, to "zero-cost" proxies. We show that certain families of predictors can be combined to achieve even better predictive power.

We explore in depth an approach to few shot visual classification: complementing the few available training images with ad-hoc simulated data. We analyze the effect of different axes of variation in simulation, such as pose, lighting, model, and simulation method, and we prescribe best practices for efficiently incorporating simulated data for real-world performance gain. Our experiments reveal: (1)synthetic data can considerably reduce error rates for rare classes, (2) as the amount of simulated data is increased, accuracy on the target class improves, and (3) high variation of simulated data provides maximum performance gain.

Many ML model explainers have been proposed to produce feature importance scores for a specific prediction. Fair evaluation of these explainers usually require estimating conditional distributions of datasets and is thus prohibitive for real datasets. In contrast, many synthetic datasets allow efficient computation of conditional distributions. We propose XAI-Bench: a suite of synthetic datasets along with a library for benchmarking feature attribution algorithms. We show the power of our library by benchmarking popular explainers across several evaluation metrics and identifying surprising failure modes.
